# Kid-Friendly Exercises

Exercises should be printable and answerable from the atlas pages. Good prompts include finding landmarks, identifying travel direction, measuring approximate distance, counting bridges, spotting rivers, and predicting the next page.

Challenge pages should use checkboxes, short blanks, and simple drawing prompts. Avoid long instruction blocks on map pages; reserve instructions for worksheet pages or a parent guide.

Difficulty can scale by age: young kids identify symbols and directions; older kids estimate scale, bearings, and route alternatives.
