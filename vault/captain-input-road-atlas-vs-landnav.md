# Captain Input — Road Atlas vs Land Nav

An Army captain (a friend of the project owner) was asked for input on whether the Army land navigation manual is a good starting point for Journey Book. This note captures the source conversation faithfully.

## The Owner's Framing (verbatim)

> "In an effort to not have to answer how close we are, on every road trip as a side project I want to put together some paper maps for the girls. Goal is that they must find where we are and figure out how much longer it will be. I am planning to build an app where I can basically make customish road atlases or grid maps. I was thinking the army Land Nav manual might be a good starting place as eventually this would roll into our hiking trips as well: https://www.armywriter.com/board/references/TC3-25x26-Part1.pdf . Wanted to get your opinion on it and if it would be too complex for what I am trying to do. Army land nav speaks a different language and MGRS maps are great, but it is a learning curve. Then you also have to get MGRS maps, which tbh I'm not sure how to get."

## The Captain's Response

The captain pointed to the **Army Publishing Directorate (APD, https://armypubs.army.mil/)** as the better, canonical, open-source home for Army doctrine — "basically all army doctrine that's open source" — rather than the third-party armywriter.com PDF. See [[MGRS USNG Map Acquisition]] and [[TC 3-25.26 Part 1 Map Reading and Land Navigation]].

## The Question This Raises

The owner names the core design tension himself: **MGRS maps are great, but Army land nav "speaks a different language" and is a learning curve** — and he is not even sure how to obtain MGRS maps. The girls' actual job is concrete and kid-sized: *find where we are* and *figure out how much longer it will be*.

This forces a choice the rest of the research answers:
- Should the maps be more **road-atlas** (familiar, kid-readable, find-our-spot / how-much-longer), OR
- Should there be a **deliberate learning curve** that starts digestible and progressively builds toward real Army land nav (MGRS, [[TC 3-25.26 Part 1 Map Reading and Land Navigation]]) so the kids eventually learn it without being overwhelmed?

The recommendation is developed in [[Land Nav Learning Curve Recommendation]], scaffolded by [[Progressive Map Skills Curriculum]] and [[Orienteering Course Levels As Scaffold]], and the "how do I get MGRS maps" question is answered in [[MGRS USNG Map Acquisition]].

## Two Open Sub-Points

- **Source of truth for doctrine:** prefer APD over armywriter.com. APD hosts TC 3-25.26 officially (PUB_ID 103749).
- **Acquisition gap:** the owner's "not sure how to get [MGRS maps]" is a real, answerable blocker — Journey Book can *generate its own grid* rather than sourcing pre-made military maps. See [[MGRS USNG Map Acquisition]].
